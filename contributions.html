Volunteering

  • COVID19
  • Floods
  • Disability

By participating in establishing and coordinating roles in the Sudanese Team for Response and Crisis Management, which is a volunteer team specializing in disaster management, working in times of extreme and urgent need to coordinate efforts and reinforce tools, standards and policies and direct them in the right direction to make the largest positive impact in resisting the disaster. The team raises the slogan: “Survive Together”.

In the fall of 2013, the torrents raided many areas in Sudan, causing great damage to local communities, which invited a large group of young people estimated at thousands of different specialties to organize in an emergency volunteer activity to relieve the affected people : "Nafeer Initiative".
I participated in the technical work team that built an ambulatory technical toolkit to use in averting the disaster.

By working as a volunteer developer with the Abrar Organization for the Care of the Handicapped and War Mine Action, which is a non-governmental organization that aims to combat anti-personnel landmines that represent a major cause of large numbers of disabilities in Sudan.
The organization also works with the legislative and legal authorities in Sudan to enact legislation and laws that facilitate and reserved the rights of persons with disabilities.
